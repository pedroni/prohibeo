Prohibeo is built for people who still need the internet.

Not less internet. Better defaults.

Most focus tools overcorrect. They treat the browser like the problem, then try to
solve it by shutting everything down. That can work for a sprint. It rarely fits a
real day.

Real work happens in the same browser that holds docs, research, messages, tutorials,
feeds, and every easy detour. The goal is not to pretend that tension does not exist.
The goal is to shape it.

## The idea

Prohibeo keeps three controls close at hand:

- block a site when it rarely helps
- hide distracting sections when the site is useful but the page is noisy
- run a schedule when the rule only matters during certain hours

That mix matters. Some sites deserve a hard stop. Others just need a cleaner surface.
Often the best fix is not less access. It is less bait.

## What this blog is for

This blog is for people who want a browser that feels quieter and easier to use with
intent.

You will find short guides on things like:

- blocking distracting sites without breaking your workflow
- using YouTube for learning without falling into recommendations
- setting rules that support work, study, and low-energy days
- making mixed-use sites less grabby

The tone will stay simple: less guilt, less hustle, more practical control.

## What Prohibeo is not

It is not a discipline test.

It is not a life system.

It is not a promise that software can fix attention by itself.

It is a small layer of structure inside Chrome. Enough to remove the obvious traps.
Enough to make the better next click easier.

If that sounds useful, start with the guides, then try Prohibeo in Chrome.
